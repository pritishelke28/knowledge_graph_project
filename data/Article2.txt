Alphabet Inc. was created in 2015 as a massive holding company and technology conglomerate to organize its sprawling corporate structure. Alphabet owns Google, which remains the absolute leader in global web search infrastructure and online advertising. Under this corporate umbrella, Google owns YouTube, the world's most dominant video-sharing and streaming media platform. 

Looking back at strategic tech history, Google acquired YouTube in 2006 for 1.65 billion dollars in stock, which is widely considered one of the most successful acquisitions of the digital era. Managing this global tech empire requires heavy corporate leadership, and executive Sundar Pichai serves as the joint CEO of both Alphabet and its primary subsidiary, Google. The conglomerate continues to invest billions into artificial intelligence, cloud infrastructure, and autonomous driving.
